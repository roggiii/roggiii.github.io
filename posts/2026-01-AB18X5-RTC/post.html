AB18X5 Real Time Clock

Specifically AB1815.

Goals

  • As low as possible power consumption
  • Disconnect MCU when not in use, not only wake up over interrupt
  • High clock accuracy is not critical; should be able to wake up MCU every 30 seconds (can deviate a few percent)
  • Save data from MCU to internal RTC RAM

Footprint

The Datasheet is best downloaded directly from the manufacturer Abracon, because usual sources like Mouser or Digikey only provide an abbreviated version.

The AB18X5 comes in a 16-pin QFN package, which is only 3×3 mm in size. Soldering with a hot air gun should be possible. The thermal pad on the bottom side needs to be connected to ground.

All footprints I found online were not suitable. The closest standard library part QFN-16-1EP_3x3mm_P0.5mm_EP1.7x1.7mm was also not ideal. So I decided to make my own.

Since I find it quite tedious to draw footprints directly in KiCad, I usually do it in Fusion 360 and then export a .dxf file.
