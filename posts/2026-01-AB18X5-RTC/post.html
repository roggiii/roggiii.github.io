AB18X5 Real Time Clock

Design Requirements

  • As low as possible power consumption
  • Completly disconnect MCU form voltage supply via mosfett when not in use
  • High clock accuracy is not critical; should be able to wake up MCU every 30 seconds for a measurement cycle
  • Save data from MCU to internal RTC RAM

The AB1805 Variant has I²C and the AB1815 SPI. Since the application does nothing time sensitive, the higher speed of SPI is not neccecary.

Footprint

The Datasheet is best downloaded directly from the manufacturer Abracon, because usual sources like Mouser or Digikey only provide an abbreviated version. The AB18X5 comes in a 16-pin QFN package, which is only 3×3 mm in size. Soldering with a hot air gun should be possible. The thermal pad on the bottom side needs to be connected to ground. All footprints I found online were not suitable. The closest standard library part QFN-16-1EP_3x3mm_P0.5mm_EP1.7x1.7mm was also not ideal. So I decided to make my own in Fusion 360.
